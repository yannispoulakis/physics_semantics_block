Service Semantics

In computer science and information science, an ontology encompasses a representation, formal naming, and definition of the categories, properties, and relations between the concepts, data, and entities that substantiate one, many, or all domains of discourse. More simply, an ontology is a way of showing the properties of a subject area and how they are related, by defining a set of concepts and categories that represent the subject. In PHYSICS the service semantics ontology aims to describe resources in a multi-cluster scenario. Each cluster is defined by functional and non-functional properties such as CPU, Ram , etc per node and properties such as SLA governing a cloud service provisioning, benchmarks etc.
